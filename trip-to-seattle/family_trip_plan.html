Top Attractions for Kids (6-9 years)

Must-Visit Family Attractions
Pacific Science Center

Interactive science exhibits perfect for curious children, featuring a Tropical Butterfly House, hands-on activities, and educational demonstrations. Kids can learn while having fun!

Rating: 4.1/5 | Time needed: 3-4 hours

Woodland Park Zoo

Award-winning zoo with over 1,000 animals and a dedicated children's area. Kids can see animals from around the world in naturalistic habitats and even pet some friendly creatures.

Rating: 4.3/5 | Time needed: 3-5 hours

Museum of Flight

The world's largest air and space museum where kids can climb aboard aircraft, use flight simulators, and learn about space exploration. Perfect for young aviation enthusiasts!

Rating: 4.7/5 | Time needed: 3-4 hours

Seattle Aquarium

Located on the waterfront, this aquarium focuses on hands-on marine experiences with touch pools, underwater viewing domes, and daily feedings. Kids can see sea otters, octopuses, and more!

Rating: 4.0/5 | Time needed: 2-3 hours

Pike Place Market

Historic market with flying fish, street performers, and unique shops. Kids love watching fishmongers throw fish and exploring the quirky shops and food stalls.

Rating: 4.5/5 | Time needed: 2-3 hours

Space Needle

Iconic Seattle landmark with glass floors and observation deck offering 360째 views. Children are fascinated by the elevator ride and seeing the city from above!

Rating: 4.3/5 | Time needed: 1-2 hours

Additional Kid-Friendly Activities
Alki Beach

2.5-mile beach strip with views of downtown, playgrounds, and shallow water for wading. Great for a relaxed family day.

Washington State Ferries

Take a scenic ferry ride across Puget Sound to Bainbridge Island (30 min) for amazing views and a fun day trip.

Museum of Illusions

Interactive exhibits with optical illusions that will amaze and entertain children of all ages.

Museum of Pop Culture

Unique museum with interactive music exhibits where kids can test their DJ skills and see memorabilia.

Discovery Park

534-acre nature park with trails, beaches, and wildlife - perfect for active kids who need to burn energy.

Seattle Mariners Game

If timing works, catch a baseball game at T-Mobile Park for a quintessential American experience.

Transportation in Seattle

Transportation Options:
Public Transportation
  • Link Light Rail: Connects airport to downtown
  • King County Metro: Bus network throughout the city
  • Seattle Streetcar: Limited routes in downtown area
  • Monorail: Connects Seattle Center to downtown
  • Cost: $2.75-$3.25 per ride (children under 6 ride free)
  • ORCA Card: Reloadable transit pass for all services
Rideshare & Taxi
  • Uber/Lyft: Widely available throughout Seattle
  • Taxis: Available at airport, downtown hotels
  • Benefits: Convenient with children, no waiting
  • Cost: More expensive than public transit but saves time
  • Note: Request vehicles with car seats if needed
Water Transportation
  • Water Taxi: Downtown to West Seattle/Alki Beach
  • Washington State Ferries: Service to nearby islands
  • Cost: $4-$9 per adult, reduced fares for children
  • Experience: Fun transportation option and sightseeing
Transportation Tips for Families
  • From airport to downtown: Take Link Light Rail for affordability or taxi/rideshare for convenience after long flight
  • Consider hotel location in relation to attractions to minimize transit time
  • For families with young children, rideshare apps often provide the most convenient option despite higher cost
  • Purchase an ORCA card if planning to use public transit frequently
  • Plan buffer time for transit with children - allow extra time for connections
  • Water taxis and ferries are both transportation and fun experiences for kids

Family-Friendly Dining

Seattle offers many dining options that cater to families with children. Here are some recommendations:

Casual Dining Options
  • Pike Place Chowder - Award-winning chowder in Pike Place Market
  • Ivar's Acres of Clams - Seafood restaurant on the waterfront with children's menu
  • Mod Pizza - Build-your-own pizza, perfect for picky eaters
  • Portage Bay Cafe - Breakfast/brunch with "breakfast bar" of toppings
  • Dick's Drive-In - Seattle burger institution
Special Treats
  • Molly Moon's Ice Cream - Local favorite with unique flavors
  • The Crumpet Shop - Pike Place Market institution
  • Top Pot Doughnuts - Seattle's famous hand-forged doughnuts
  • Ellenos Real Greek Yogurt - Fresh yogurt bar at Pike Place
  • Shug's Soda Fountain - Old-fashioned ice cream parlor
Dining Tips with Children
  • Dine early (5-6pm) to avoid crowds and long waits
  • Many Seattle restaurants source local ingredients - an opportunity to introduce children to fresh foods
  • Food courts in Pike Place Market and various shopping centers offer variety for different preferences
  • Pack snacks for busy sightseeing days to avoid hungry, cranky children
  • Seattle has numerous Asian restaurants that might be familiar to children from Shanghai
  • Most family-friendly attractions have on-site dining options

Money
  • Currency: US Dollar (USD)
  • Credit Cards: Widely accepted (Visa, Mastercard, etc.)
  • ATMs: Available throughout Seattle

Communication
  • WiFi: Available at most hotels, cafes, and attractions
  • SIM Cards: Available at airport, major retailers (T-Mobile, AT&T)
  • Emergency Number: 911 for all emergencies
Health & Safety
  • Hospitals: Several major hospitals in Seattle area

  • Safety: Seattle is generally safe in tourist areas
