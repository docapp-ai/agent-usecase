Tips for Visiting Seattle with Kids

  • Plan around energy levels - Schedule more active attractions in the morning when kids have more energy
  • Use public transportation - Kids often enjoy riding buses, light rail, and especially the monorail
  • Pack snacks and water - Keep hungry moments at bay with portable snacks
  • Check for special events - Many museums have special kids' programming in summer
  • Take breaks - Plan downtime between attractions to prevent overstimulation
  • Be prepared for weather changes - Even in August, it's good to have light jackets
  • Get CityPASS - Consider the Seattle CityPASS for savings on multiple attractions
  • Ask about kids' activities - Many attractions have scavenger hunts or special programs for children

10:00 AM - 5:00 PM $19.75-$24.75 4.1/5

Interactive science center offering hands-on exhibits, a planetarium, butterfly house, and IMAX theater. Perfect for curious kids who like to touch, explore, and experiment!

9:30 AM - 6:00 PM (Summer) $15.75-$22.75 4.3/5

9404 E Marginal Way S, Seattle, WA 98108

10:00 AM - 5:00 PM $17-$25 4.7/5

The largest private air and space museum in the world with over 175 aircraft and spacecraft. Kids can sit in cockpits, explore Air Force One, and try flight simulators!
